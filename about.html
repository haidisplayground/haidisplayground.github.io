Where am I?

You are in a free playground, and all kind things are allowed, especially pets.

I'm Haidi. I make things across a handful of mediums, without rules. That is usually where it gets interesting.

I shoot on film and on a small Nikon, paint in acrylics and watercolour, and draw on an iPad. Each one pulls something different out of me.

There is sound in here too. I sing, I play a few instruments more or less well, and every so often a song escapes into the world under the name chadeaux_. It comes from the same place as everything else, a need to get the thing I am hearing in my head to exist outside it.

For a long time all of this lived in drawers, on camera rolls, and in finished private files. This site is the wall I finally hung it on. A place to let the grain show.

The other half of my life is neuroscience. I trained in Glasgow and London, and I spend most of my time on questions about how the brain decides, feels, and regulates itself: why moods rise and fall, why we put off the things we most want to do, how we weigh effort against reward. It is not as far from the art as it sounds. Both are really about paying close attention to inner life and trying to make it legible to someone else. The art just gives me the focus I need for reading papers.
